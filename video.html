What is music

Music is the art of organizing sound in time, typically involving elements such as melody, harmony, rhythm, timbre, texture, and dynamics. It is generally defined as humanly-organized sound, usually created for the purpose of emotional expression or achieving beauty. This organization can include both sounds and deliberate silences, which together shape the music's form and tension. At its core, music is a cultural universal present in all human societies, serving as a means of personal expression, cultural preservation, and communication.

Pop music

Popular music genre originating in the late 1950s, melodic and accessible, often combining elements from other genres

Hip hop

Born in the 1970s in the U.S., characterized by rapping, electronic beats, and cultural elements like graffiti and breakdancing

Rock

Emerged in the 1960s from rock and roll, typically using electric guitars, bass, and drums, with energetic rhythms
